About Me

My name is Isabella Cattalini, and I am currently studying to become a secondary school teacher, specialising in Technology and Applied Studies at ACU. Before stepping into teaching, I pursued my passion for fashion and design at UTS, studying a Bachelor of Fashion and Design for 1 year in 2022. This experience taught me the importance of creativity, problem-solving, and visual learning as powerful tools for expression. The decision to study teaching came from a combination of my design background and the influence of inspiring educators throughout my schooling, particularly my Year 12 Textiles teacher, whose encouragement and belief in me showed how impactful a dedicated teacher can be. My goal now is to combine my love for design with teaching, creating engaging and meaningful learning experiences that inspire students to see their own creative potential.

Me on a Personal Level

Outside of my studies, I run Bellistrated, my own design and illustration business, where I create custom artwork, stationery, and event pieces. I’ve always loved art, sewing, fashion, and all things design, and I enjoy bringing creativity into both my professional and personal life. When I’m not designing, I treasure spending time with family and friends, and I feel most at home when I’m around the water on sunny days. Creativity and connection are what drive me, and I try to live in a way that blends both into everything I do.

My Moto

This quote captures the essence of my philosophy as both a designer and a teacher. To me, design has never been just about creating something that looks good, it’s about problem-solving, functionality, and shaping meaningful experiences. In teaching, I see the same principle: my role is not only to help students create visually appealing work but to equip them with creative thinking skills that allow them to find solutions and adapt to challenges beyond the classroom. It is important to me because it reflects the lasting impact I want to have on my students’ futures, encouraging them to design not only for today but to contribute to the world they will one day lead and influence.
