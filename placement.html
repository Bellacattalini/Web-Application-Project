As part of my teaching degree at Australian Catholic University, I have undertaken 3 professional placements that were allocated by the university. Each placement was completed over a three-week period during my second and third year of study. These experiences gave me the opportunity to engage with diverse school communities, apply my developing skills in real classrooms, and refine my teaching practice under the guidance of experienced mentors.

Campbeltown Performing Arts High School

My first placement was at Campbelltown Performing Arts High School, where I taught Food Technology classes across Years 7–10. The school placed a strong emphasis on student wellbeing, with classrooms often focused on supporting students to overcome behavioural challenges through a complex range of strategies. I worked with students with diverse learning needs, including ADHD and other cognitive differences, which taught me the importance of personalised approaches and creating inclusive learning environments. This experience helped me develop greater confidence in differentiation, classroom management, and building positive relationships with students.

Emmaus Catholic College

My second placement was at Emmaus Catholic College, where I primarily taught Year 7 Textiles. Students were completing their end-of-year practical project, designing and creating plush toys, while also balancing their theory work and portfolio requirements. I developed a scaffolded schedule to support students in managing their time and meeting assessment outcomes, as well as designing learning templates that allowed them to experiment with new stitching skills and techniques. During practical lessons, I guided students through processes such as blanket stitch, basic stitch, long stitch, button sewing, and appliqué, ensuring they gained both technical proficiency and confidence in their creative work.

Mary Mackillop Catholic College

My most recent placement (2025) was at Mary MacKillop Catholic College, an all-girls school that provided me with a very different environment from my earlier experiences. The calmer classroom setting required me to adapt my teaching style and refine new approaches to suit the needs of the students. I taught Food Technology across Years 8–10, focusing primarily on theory-based lessons. This experience strengthened my ability to deliver engaging content without the support of practical lessons, encouraging me to use creative strategies to maintain student interest and ensure curriculum outcomes were met.
